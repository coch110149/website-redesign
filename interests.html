Interests!

There are a lot of things that I like. These things shape who I am, so I thought they deserved their own page.

Travel

When I was a child, my mom would travel a lot for work. The company she worked for built Lowe’s stores around the country, and she would supervise the progress. She also took her boss’s classic cars to car shows around the country. Most of these trip I got to go with her. Because of this, I have traveled most of the country and have developed a love for the uniqueness of the different parts of this country. I saw more of the country when I moved from Louisiana to California; my family drove around the country and went to 21 different states. The beauty this country offers is underappreciated. In high school, I traveled to the UK and Ireland, and I fell in love with Dublin. I would not mind moving there. It’s hard to pick favorite places, but I really enjoyed:

  • Montana, it was so quiet
  • Philadelphia, because of the history
  • Grand Canyon, seeing it in person is breathtaking
  • Hawaii, because, well, Hawaii.
  • Colorado (in summer), the mountains and the climate are perfect.
  • Dublin, Wales, and Glasgow.
  • Big Springs, Van Buren, Mo

Technology

As you might have guessed I am a big fan of Technology. I am an Android fan, but I don’t hate Apple. I am also a big fan of robotic. I cannot wait for self driving cars, I am working on building a robot that will open and close windows and blinds for me based on interior vs exterior temperature and lighting. I joke with my wife that instead of children, we can just make robots. She does not think that is funny. I’m also big into energy conservation. We ride our bikes almost everywhere in town and we do our laundry and dishes by hand. I am slowly converting what we can in our apartment over to solar power; so far it’s been lighting, phones, and fans. I’m afraid to put a panel in that is too big because of vandalism. Of course I love programming and computers, and as you might know this passion designates me as the family tech support agent, but it’s okay I enjoy it.

Cycling

Over the last year, I have become a HUGE fan and advocate of cycling. I bought my first bike shop bike 7/3/14. It is a Trek FX 7.2 with disc brakes. It may not be top of the line, but I love it. It has become my primary form of transportation. It has enabled me to see neighboring towns to me. I have put several thousand miles on it since I got it, but she is still running strong. I plan on keeping it until I graduate college, and then I want a touring bike. Amy and I have been talking about touring parts of the country on a bike, but our current bikes are not the best for that. We do have some overnight camping trips planned when it cools down a bit. I will keep you updated on those.
